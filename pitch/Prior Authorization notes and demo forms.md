###   **What is Prior Authorization?**

Prior authorization is a health insurance requirement where providers must obtain advance approval from the insurance company before delivering certain medical services, procedures, or medications to ensure they will be covered and paid for.

It's a cost-control process intended to reduce waste and limit unnecessary services [AJMC](https://www.ajmc.com/view/prior-authorizations-and-the-adverse-impact-on-continuity-of-care), though it now applies to many routine treatments beyond just expensive interventions. Prior authorization forms are inconsistent in the information required depending on the procedure requested, the insurance company and insurance companies frequently change the forms. ### **Who prepares prior authorization requests?**

Physicians complete an average of 39 prior authorization requests per week, requiring 13 hours of staff time [Surescripts](https://surescripts.com/prior-authorization-challenges-data). The responsibility varies by practice size and resources.

**In smaller practices:** Physicians often handle much of the PA work themselves, including form completion, documentation gathering, and follow-up.

**In larger practices:** Approximately 40% of physicians have dedicated staff members who work exclusively on prior authorization tasks [American Medical Association](https://www.ama-assn.org/practice-management/prior-authorization/pains-prior-authorization-create-pressure-reform)[American College of Physicians](https://www.acponline.org/advocacy/state-health-policy/toolkit-addressing-the-administrative-burden-of-prior-authorization). Nurses, practice managers, and billing coordinators typically handle the administrative workflows [Promantra](https://promantra.us/blog/prior-authorization-burden/).

**Physicians always handle:**

* Clinical documentation and medical necessity justification  
* Signing PA request forms  
* Peer-to-peer reviews with insurance medical directors

**Support staff handle:**

* Form completion and data entry  
* Submission and tracking  
* Follow-up communications with payers

**Sources**: American Medical Association, Surescripts, Promantra

### **What systems are involved?**

**1\. Electronic Health Records (EHR):** EHR systems integrate with electronic prior authorization tools, allowing staff to be guided through entering required clinical details and submitting attachments directly from the patient record [Surescripts](https://surescripts.com/what-we-do/electronic-prior-authorization).

**2\. Practice Management (PM) Systems:** Authorization verification tools integrate with PM systems to instantly analyze orders and determine if authorization is required [Waystar](https://www.waystar.com/our-platform/financial-clearance/authorizations/).

**3\. Payer Portals:** 39% of prior authorizations are done through payer portals or interactive voice response systems, requiring separate logins for each health plan [TechTarget](https://www.techtarget.com/revcyclemanagement/answer/From-AI-to-Regulation-Making-Progress-with-the-Prior-Authorization-Process).
